Prepositions of Place

Let's learn how to describe exactly where objects are located with many examples!

The Question: Where?

Prepositions of place answer the question:
"Where is it?"

They show the relationship between people, objects, or animals and their position in space.

1. IN

Use IN when something is inside a container or an enclosed area.

Examples:

  • "The ball is in the box."
  • "The keys are in my pocket."
  • "The coffee is in the cup."

2. ON

Use ON when something is touching a surface.

Examples:

  • "The book is on the table."
  • "The clock is on the wall."
  • "The cat is on the sofa."

3. UNDER

Use UNDER when something is at a lower level than something else.

Examples:

  • "The cat is under the chair."
  • "The shoes are under the bed."
  • "The ball is under the desk."

4. NEXT TO

Use NEXT TO when something is beside another object.

Examples:

  • "The apple is next to the computer."
  • "The bank is next to the supermarket."
  • "She is sitting next to her friend."

5. BEHIND

Use BEHIND when something is at the back of another object.

Examples:

  • "The dog is behind the sofa."
  • "The sun is behind the clouds."
  • "The car is behind the truck."

6. IN FRONT OF

Use IN FRONT OF when something is in a forward position.

Examples:

  • "The pencil is in front of the notebook."
  • "The teacher is in front of the class."
  • "There is a park in front of my house."

7. BETWEEN

Use BETWEEN when something is in the middle of two objects.

Examples:

  • "The student is between the two desks."
  • "The letter 'B' is between 'A' and 'C'."
  • "The café is between the library and the cinema."

8. ABOVE

Use ABOVE when something is higher than another object (not touching).

Examples:

  • "The clock is above the whiteboard."
  • "The plane is above the clouds."
  • "The shelves are above the sink."

9. ACROSS FROM / OPPOSITE

Use ACROSS FROM when two things are facing each other on different sides.

Examples:

  • "The bakery is across from the park."
  • "The hospital is across from the station."
  • "They live across from each other."

10. NEAR

Use NEAR when something is close to another object, but not touching.

Examples:

  • "The school is near my house."
  • "The remote is near the TV."
  • "Is there a pharmacy near here?"

11. AT

Use AT to talk about a specific point or location.

Examples:

  • "She is waiting at the bus stop."
  • "He is at the door."
  • "They are at the cinema."
