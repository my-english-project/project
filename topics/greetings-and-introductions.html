Goal for Today

Goal for Today

  • Learn how to greet people formally and informally.
  • Learn how to introduce yourself.
  • Learn how to introduce others.
  • Ask and answer basic personal information questions.

Greetings (Salutations)

Always use a formal greeting when meeting someone for the first time in a professional setting.

Situation Formal (Business/Elders) Informal (Friends/Family)
Greeting Good morning / afternoon / evening. Hi / Hey!
Asking How? How are you? / How have you been? What's up? / How's it going?
Response I'm doing well, thank you. And you? I'm great! / Good / Not bad.
Closing It's a pleasure to meet you. Nice to see you! / Catch you later.

Introducing Yourself

Start a conversation with someone you don't know.

Function Phrase Example
Initiate Hello. My name is... / I'm... Hello. My name is David Miller.
Ask Name And what is your name? / And you are? And you are?
Response My name is... / I'm... I'm Sarah Johnson.
Show Pleasure It's nice to meet you. It's a pleasure to meet you, Sarah.

Introducing Others

When you are the link between two people.

Type Phrase Example
Formal I'd like to introduce... "Maria, I'd like to introduce Mr. Jones, my colleague."
Informal Meet... / This is... "Meet Alex. He's a friend from my class."
Acknowledging (Response) "Nice to meet you, Mr. Jones."

Asking for Basic Information (Part 1)

Keep the conversation going.

Where are you from?
I'm from Brazil. / I grew up in London.
What do you do?
I'm a student. / I work as an accountant.
What are your hobbies?
I enjoy hiking and reading. / I like video games.
How old are you?
I am 25 years old.

Asking for Basic Information (Part 2)

Do you have any brothers or sisters?
Yes, I have one brother. / No, I am an only child.
What is your favorite food?
My favorite food is pizza. / I really like sushi.
Do you like learning English?
Yes, I love it! / It's difficult, but I like it.
What time do you start class?
I start class at 8:30 in the morning.
