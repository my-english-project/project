Usage & Meaning

We use the **Simple Present** for:

Habits & Routines

"I drink coffee every morning."

General Truths

"The sun rises in the east."

Fixed Timetables

"The train leaves at 8:00 PM."

Permanent Situations

"I live in Salamanca."

Affirmative: He / She / It

The main rule: add -s to the verb.

Ending of Verb Rule Examples
Most verbs Add -s Work → Works / Eat → Eats
-ch, -sh, -s, -x, -z, -o Add -es Watch → Watches / Go → Goes
Consonant + y Change to -ies Study → Studies / Fly → Flies
Vowel + y Just add -s Play → Plays / Buy → Buys

Negative Form

We use auxiliary verbs DO or DOES + NOT.

I / You / We / They

DON'T

"I don't like Mondays."

He / She / It

DOESN'T

"She doesn't like Mondays."

⚠️ IMPORTANT: When using "doesn't", the verb returns to its base form (NO 'S').
Wrong: She doesn't plays. → Correct: She doesn't play.

Questions & Short Answers

DO / DOES + Subject + Verb (Base)?

Do you live here?

Yes, I do. / No, I don't.

Does he work today?

Yes, he does. / No, he doesn't.

Night

Have dinner

Take a shower

Example Sentences:

  • • I wake up at 6:30 every morning.
  • • My father brushes his teeth after breakfast. (3rd person)
  • • We have lunch in the cafeteria at 2:00 PM.
  • • Sarah does her homework in the library. (3rd person)
  • • They don't go to sleep late on weekdays.
