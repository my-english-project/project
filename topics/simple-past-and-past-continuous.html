Telling Stories in English

Past Continuous vs. Simple Past

The Two Key Players

1. The Simple Past

📷 Like a photograph. Completed actions at a specific time. The "main events".

Example: Yesterday, I walked to school.

2. The Past Continuous

📹 Like a video. Actions in progress over a period of time. Setting the scene.

Example: At 8:00 AM, I was walking to school.

The "Interruption" Concept

We often use them together: The Past Continuous is the long background action, and the Simple Past is the short action that interrupts it.

"I was sleeping (long) when the alarm rang (short)."

Time Clauses (Connectors)

We use "The Fantastic Four" to link these actions:

  • WHEN + Simple Past: Usually introduces the short interruption.
    We were watching a movie when the pizza arrived.
  • WHILE + Past Continuous: Emphasizes the duration of a long action.
    The phone rang while I was taking a shower.
  • BEFORE & AFTER (+ Simple Past): Used for sequencing two completed events.
    I checked my email before I started work.
