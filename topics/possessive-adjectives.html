Possessive adjectives are words that show ownership or possession. They indicate who something belongs to and are always followed by a noun.

Key Rule: Always Before a Noun

Possessive adjectives (*my, your, his, her, its, our, their*) function as adjectives‚Äîthey modify a noun.

Example: My jacket is red. / Their house is big.

Agreement: Owner, Not Object

The adjective must agree with the owner, not the item being owned. The form of the adjective never changes.

Compare: Her book (singular object) / Her books (plural objects).

Key Takeaway: Adjectives vs. Pronouns

Always distinguish between adjectives (which require a noun) and pronouns (which stand alone).

Owner Possessive Adjective Possessive Pronoun
I MY (My coat) MINE (The coat is mine)
We OUR (Our idea) OURS (The idea is ours)

Possessive Adjective forms: my, your, his, her, its, our, their. Remember: Adjectives must modify a noun!
