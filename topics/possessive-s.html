We use the **apostrophe s ('s)**, also called possessive 's, as a determiner to show that something belongs to someone or something.

Basic Ownership

The apostrophe is placed on the owner, and the item follows.

Is that **Olivia's** bag? [cite: 4]

**Britain's** coastline is very beautiful. [cite: 5]

Complex Noun Phrases

The possessive 's can be used in complex noun phrases [cite: 8] and can even appear twice in the same phrase[cite: 10].

Greg is her **youngest daughter's** husband. [cite: 9]

We went to **Jake's father's** funeral. [cite: 11]

Possessive 's Rules: Singular vs. Plural

The position of the apostrophe depends entirely on whether the noun is singular or plural[cite: 17].

Singular Noun

Use **'s** after a singular noun[cite: 17, 23].

FORM: Noun + **'s**

Example: The **girl's** bedroom (one girl) [cite: 25, 26]

Regular Plural Noun

Use **'** after a plural noun that ends in **-s**[cite: 17, 24].

FORM: Plural Noun + **'**

Example: The **girls'** bedroom (more than one girl) [cite: 27, 28]

Irregular Plural Noun

Use **'s** with irregular plural nouns (e.g., children, men)[cite: 19].

FORM: Irregular Noun + **'s**

Example: The **children's** parents... [cite: 20]

Example: other **people's** property. [cite: 21]

Possessive 's for Time and Duration

We also use possessive 's to talk about time and duration[cite: 12].

Time (Periods)

Used for specific time units like days, weeks, or months.

Is that **yesterday's** paper? [cite: 13]

Duration (Length of Time)

Used to express how long something lasts.

I've only had one **week's** holiday so far this year. [cite: 14]

Summary: Final Check

Always check the form of the possessor to decide where the apostrophe goes.

Possessor Form Rule Example
Singular Noun Add **'s** [cite: 23] The **girl's** bedroom [cite: 25]
Regular Plural Noun (ends in -s) Add only **'** [cite: 24] The **girls'** bedroom [cite: 27]
Irregular Plural Noun (e.g. *children, men*) Add **'s** [cite: 19] The **children's** parents [cite: 20]

Key Takeaway: The possessive 's can also be used for time, duration [cite: 12], and in complex noun phrases[cite: 8].
